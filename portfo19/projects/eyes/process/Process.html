This project began when I was prompted to perform a “decolonizing gesture” in my Decolonizing Design class at RISD. I decided to roll my eyes at both the lack of female designers in our graphic design history textbooks and the term “genius” that is consistently applied to men. At first, I designed a poster of swarming and rolling eyes over the text “he was a genius let me explain it to you.” The poster format didn’t seem subversive enough as a critique of graphic design, so I decided to create a public installation instead. I shot an up-close video of myself rolling my eyes and chose the best still frames to print. I then incorporated examples of sexist things I have rolled my eyes at. I posted the print-outs around RISD’s campus and then stealthily wheat-pasted some around Downtown Providence. For the final installation I printed the images at a much larger scale. I included a written statement on the wall next to the work and created a small accompanying publication.
