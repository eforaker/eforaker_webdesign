The Double Standards Manual examines sexist double standards that I and many other children experienced while growing up. Childhood is when these gender-based double standards were most strictly imposed upon me (both explicitly and subconsciously). The notion of strict brand identity guidelines as the be-all end-all is inherently flawed because it denies the reality of an unpredictable and evolving world. Flexibility is needed to create good design on a case-to-case basis. Similarly, strict gender roles limit one’s potential. As I built this collection, patterns of color, typography, and graphic elements emerged, separating products marketed for boys versus girls. I emphasized stereotypes by applying the generic female graphic qualities to male gendered logotypes, and vice versa.
