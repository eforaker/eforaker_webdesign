Given the prompt to create a book based off of a single block, I chose a section of Sheldon Street on the East Side of Providence. I first took pictures of every letter or number and transcribed the text. I experimented with recreating the typography of gas meters and drew the shapes of wherever I found the text (door, sticker, meter, tag, etc.). I then liberally sorted all of the shapes into one of five categories: horizontal rectangular, vertical rectangular, square, circular, or miscellaneous. I organized the book by placing each shape directly preceding the text found on that shape. I experimented with only having one letter or number per page, but that resulted in a monster book over two thousand pages long. Instead, I put the text on a grid across the spreads, so there would be six characters per page. Spaces were included in-between words. I then skewed each text character in Illustrator to create the effect of viewing text at an angle, just as I had done while collecting this content. The words and phrases remain barely legible across the spreads.
