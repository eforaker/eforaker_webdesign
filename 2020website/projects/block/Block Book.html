Given the prompt to create a book with content collected on a single block in Providence, I chose a section of Sheldon Street (the block I lived on). I began by photographing every letter or number on the entire block and transcribing this text. I also drew the shape of the element the text was found on (gas meter, stop sign, sticker, etc).
The book is a running list of every word or phrase spelled out over the full spread. The phrase’s corresponding shape acts as punctuation by marking the end of one phrase and beginning of the next. Each character is skewed to mimick the photographs I took on the block; All of the text appeared at strange angles as I creeped around to peer into my neighbors’ driveways and yards.
