We're Depressed.

The number of Americans who say they've taken an antidepressant over the past month rose by 65% between 1999 and 2014.

Women were nearly twice as likely as men to be taking the medications. Antidepressants were used by 16.5% of females compared to just under 9% of males.

1/4 of all people [surveyed] who took antidepressants over the past month reported having taken them for 10 years or more.

Source: CBS News "Antidepressant use in U.S. soars by 65 percent in 15 years"

Published: August 16, 2017

Researchers: U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention's National Center for Health Statistics
