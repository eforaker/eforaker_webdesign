Strawberries and Champagne by Victoria’s Secret

Splashing onto the drugstore circuit of 1977, Babe has become somewhat of a perfume urban legend. Some say it’s because it carved the path for the fruity florals of now due to the unusual raspberry note for the time. Others say for the iconic Margaux Hemingway advertisements that made you want to go tubing down a river donning a glamorous chartreuse ball gown.

Either way, this is one of those affordable pieces of opulence for the teen girl who shopped at Bradlees. She’s definitely also sporting Wella Balsam shampoo and a fresh pair of clogs. Her lips are so freshly lacquered in Bonne Bell gloss you can nearly make out your reflection. She’s clompin to the mailbox to drop off her college applications because she’s cute but not kept. Remember that Babe falls in the wake of Revlon’s 1973 classic, Charlie, and is in the women’s liberation roundup of fragrances.

Notes (there are reportedly 118) include aldehydes, raspberry, bergamot, jasmine, hyacinth, ylang-ylang, lily of the valley, honey, rose, orris root, carnation, citrus, coriander, oak moss, amber, vetiver, musk and celery seed. In real life you will smell like the fragrance strip insert in Ms. Magazine.


It’s V-Day in 2004 and a secret admirer has slipped a Spongebob-themed Valentine into your locker. The note contains two simple sentences that still shake you to your earthly core: “Cheesecake Factory Tonight at 7 pm. I’ll be carrying a white rose.” You shit a brick and promptly send an SMS to every friend you have. This is more serious than recently procuring a G-string. It’s the most important moment of your life. After school, you immediately go home and begin the ritual of becoming irresistible. You lay out each tool with precision on your vanity, and start consulting the stack of teen magazines you’ve been saving for this very occasion. You study them while blasting Ashanti’s entire discography on your 1st generation iPod Mini to feel something. Each dating column leaves you more confused than the last, so you decide to use them purely for outfit inspo. This requires the big guns, ie., the iconic July 2003 edition of Vanity Fair. You land on a look reminiscent of Ashley Olsen’s empire waist cami dress over jeans. Although the magazines were 50/50 on being a complete slut vs. the understated cool girl, you know one thing’s for sure, you gotta smell like num-nums. There’s no better pairing than Strawberries and Champagne by Victoria’s Secret. You mist yourself liberally then finish the look with Candy Kisses lip balm in French Vanilla. Your mom drops you off in the parking lot as you slowly march towards the rest of your life, and Fried Macaroni and Cheese. Notes include Big Strawberry, Champagne, Black Currant, Chamomile, and Aloe Vera. In real life you will smell like the entire dessert menu. Happy Valentine’s Day to all!
